Let me share with you my experience of how working in college laid the
foundation for my future. While I was at BYU, I was a Near Eastern Studies
major and spent three years as a research assistant for a faculty member
in my department. There are two ways this job helped prepare me for my
future. In one way, l developed skills as an employee that I still use today.
I learned how to work independently on assigned projects, meet weekly deadlines,
write professionally, and understand the publication process for books and articles.

Beyond that, this professor also became somewhat of a mentor to me. He gave me
advice and helped me navigate my way to a master’s program in Jewish Studies.
It was while I was in graduate school that I decided to become a librarian. After
I graduated from my master’s program, I pulled out some of his application
suggestions again to use for library school and my applications to grad school
round 2 were even better than before. Eventually I made my way to BYU-Idaho and
became the Religion librarian based on my past education and work experience. You
never know how what you do now will directly impact your future.
